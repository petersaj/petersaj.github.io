Emergence of reproducible spatiotemporal activity during motor learning.

Peters AJ, Chen S, Komiyama T, Nature 2014
Main finding:

Activity in the motor cortex is flexibly related to movement, becoming more consistent for learned movements.

We tracked the activity of neurons in the motor cortex over weeks while mice learned a lever-press movement. Movements were accompanied by different sets of neurons active at variable times, but activity became consistent with learning. Similar movements performed early and late in learning were associated with very different activity patterns, suggesting that there is no single way for the motor cortex to produce movement, but instead neuronal populations alter their relationship to movement through learning.
