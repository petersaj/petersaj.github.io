I am a postdoc neuroscientist in the Carandini/Harris lab at University College London. I'm interested in how activity is propagated across and within brain regions to ultimately drive behavior.

In my research, I work with mice to record the activity of large populations of neurons during learning and behavior. I have used two-photon imaging, Neuropixels electrophysiology, and widefield imaging to record activity, in addition to pharmacological and optogenetic methods of activity manipulation. Investigating large-scale activity across the brain is important to shedding light on how neurons can collectively produce sensation, cognition, and behavior.
